About Me

My journey, goals, and reflections

My Journey

Hello! I am Priscilla Oluchukwu Ikeri, a passionate frontend developer with over 3 years of experience crafting digital experiences that are both beautiful and functional.

My journey in tech started during my post-NYSC days when I discovered my love for problem-solving through code. Since then, I have been on an exciting path of continuous learning and growth, working with various technologies and frameworks to bring ideas to life.

I believe in writing clean, maintainable code and creating accessible, user-friendly interfaces that make a positive impact on people's lives.

Goals in This Program

I am incredibly excited to be part of the HNG13 internship program, and I have several goals I hope to achieve:

  • Master Advanced JavaScript Concepts: Deepen my understanding of modern JavaScript patterns and best practices
  • Improve Performance Optimization: Learn advanced techniques for creating lightning-fast web applications
  • Enhance Accessibility Expertise: Become an expert in creating truly inclusive web experiences
  • Build Complex Applications: Work on challenging projects that push my skills to the next level
  • Network with Peers: Connect with other passionate developers and learn from their experiences
  • Contribute to Open Source: Gain confidence to contribute meaningfully to the developer community

Areas I am Working to Strengthen

While I am comfortable with many aspects of frontend development, I am actively working to improve in these areas:

  • Advanced CSS Architecture: Implementing scalable, maintainable CSS systems for large projects
  • State Management Complexity: Managing complex state in large-scale applications efficiently
  • Testing Strategies: Implementing comprehensive testing workflows beyond basic unit tests
  • Web Security: Deepening my understanding of modern web security practices
  • Performance Monitoring: Setting up effective performance monitoring and analytics
  • Technical Leadership: Guiding teams and making architectural decisions confidently

I see these not as weaknesses, but as opportunities for growth and areas where I can significantly level up my skills.
